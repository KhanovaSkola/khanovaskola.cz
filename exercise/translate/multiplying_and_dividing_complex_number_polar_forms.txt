233.6 	 The first number (<code><var>A_REP</var></code>) has angle <code><var>A_ANGLE_REP</var></code> and radius <code><var>A_RADIUS</var></code>.

233.7 	 The second number (<code><var>B_REP</var></code>) has angle <code><var>B_ANGLE_REP</var></code> and radius <code><var>B_RADIUS</var></code>.

233.8 	 The radius of the result will be <code><var>A_RADIUS</var> \cdot <var>B_RADIUS</var></code>, which is <code><var>ANSWER_RADIUS</var></code>.

233.9 	 The sum of the angles is <code><var>A_ANGLE_REP</var> + <var>B_ANGLE_REP</var> = <var>INTERMEDIATE_ANGLE_REP</var></code>.

233.10 	 The angle <code><var>INTERMEDIATE_ANGLE_REP</var></code> is more than <code>2 \pi</code>. A complex number goes a full circle if its angle is increased by <code>2 \pi</code>, so it
                        goes back to itself. Because of that, angles of complex numbers are convient to keep between <code>0</code> and <code>2 \pi</code>.

233.11 	 <code><var>INTERMEDIATE_ANGLE_REP</var> - 2 \pi = <var>ANSWER_ANGLE_REP</var></code>

233.17 	 Dividing complex numbers in polar forms can be done by dividing the radii
                    and subtracting the angles.

233.18 	 The first number (<code><var>A_REP</var></code>) has angle <code><var>A_ANGLE_REP</var></code> and radius <var>A_RADIUS</var>.

233.19 	 The second number (<code><var>B_REP</var></code>) has angle <code><var>B_ANGLE_REP</var></code> and radius <var>B_RADIUS</var>.

233.20 	 The radius of the result will be <code>\frac{<var>A_RADIUS</var>}{<var>B_RADIUS</var>}</code>, which is <var>ANSWER_RADIUS</var>.

233.22 	 The angle <code><var>INTERMEDIATE_ANGLE_REP</var></code> is negative. A complex number goes a full circle if its angle is increased by <code>2 \pi</code>, so it

233.24 	 The angle of the result is <code><var>A_ANGLE_REP</var> - <var>B_ANGLE_REP</var> = <var>ANSWER_ANGLE_REP</var></code>.
